Policy Management

The previous sections spoke about enabling solutions and using queries to group resources to build an organisational model. This section expands on these ideas and introduces how policies work in Conductor.

  • The general policy model

    With Conductor, solutions define policies and resource types (e.g. AWS EC2 instances) they apply to. Policies are associated with resources via resource groups, allowing policies to work well with highly ephemeral environments. For each type of policy enabled by a solution, Conductor will find what groups a resource belongs to and calculate an effective policy for that resource. When a solution is invoked (e.g. it sees the resource has changed), it will process all affected resources, along with its policy.



    1. User enables a solution and sets a policy on a resource group.


    2. Conductor uses the solution defined conflict strategy and associates an effective policy to each resource.


    3. Solution is invoked because of an event such as a resource changing, a schedule or external API invocation.


    4. Solution invocation processes all resources with a policy associated.

  • View policy coverage, adherence and compliance

    Setting policy in Conductor is simple however in larger organisational models this can still become a spaghetti junction and difficult to manage at scale - especially where many resource groups may exist. To account for this, it is possible to quickly switch views between what policies are applied overall within the organisational resource group model and show how well resources comply with them.

  • Support policy management in split-operational organisations

    Conductor is about enabling any organisation with any policy management model. Key to this is making policies a first class citizen within the Conductor framework. Because of this, policies can be inherited and merged together (as described above), making Conductor an ideal way to support scenarios where organisations may want to have multiple self-organised teams, but still retain a lean group level IT presence to govern high level policies.
